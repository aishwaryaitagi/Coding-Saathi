Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam
1931 - 2015

Biography

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, affectionately known as the "Missile Man of India," was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. He rose from humble beginnings to become one of India's most respected scientists, statesmen, and visionaries. Dr. Kalam's significant contributions to India's space and missile programs include leading the successful development of the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) and the Agni and Prithvi missiles.

As the 11th President of India (2002-2007), Dr. Kalam infused the office with warmth, accessibility, and inspiration. His presidency focused on promoting education, particularly among the underprivileged, and encouraging youth to pursue science and innovation. He dedicated his post-presidential years to interacting with students and nurturing their dreams.

Dr. Kalam's wisdom and vision are encapsulated in his books, including "Wings of Fire," an autobiographical account of his life journey, and "Ignited Minds," where he shares his thoughts on transforming India into a developed nation. His teachings continue to ignite the minds and hearts of millions.
